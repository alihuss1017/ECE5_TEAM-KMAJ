What does PID stand for? What is the purpose of the P, the I, and the D? What steps did you take to choose the best values for your robot? Explain this like you would for someone taking this course next year.

  Proportional, integral, and derivative. The proportional control is basically an amplifier for the turn rate; it can help with controlling the speed of each motor to adjust for turns depending on the error(e.g. being to the left of the track means you would need to increase the speed of the left wheel to turn left, or you could even decrease the speed of the right wheel. The integral rate uses previous errors made and adds them up in total. This helps with resolving small errors, as proportional control is rather useful for larger errors. The stored memory of these small errors can help resolve those small issues. The derivative control predicts the error in turn rate before making a turn, and using that current error with the previous error will allow you to make turns. In choosing the best values, my team and I decided to start with a derivative and integral control of 0, with a proportional control of 20. We noticed that the robot was not able to make turns, and thus amplified the derivative control to 20. This helped the robot in detecting when to make a turn. However, the turn itself was not large enough to keep it on track. In an attempt to fix this, we changed the proportional control to 40. With this, the robot was able to make a turn large enough to follow a curve-like path.
